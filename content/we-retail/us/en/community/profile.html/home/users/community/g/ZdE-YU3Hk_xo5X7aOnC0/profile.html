Hey We.Retail ! I was wondering what you thought of my suggestion: a helmet liner to cover the space between your forehead and goggles. Do you know what I mean? I have seen those helmets that have a big visor connected, but those always fog up on me. I want to be able to use my own goggles with your Kawartha Snow Helmet but I always get brain freeze.

What if you had a 1-2 inch strip of felt or some other material that can be attached to the inside of the helmet that you could fold down and place your goggles over? Ashley Thompson Replied to Electric Boots
Not a gimmick, people. They work. I was concerned enough about my toes to buy some when I hiked Kilimanjaro last year (Josh I hope you're reading this). You generally begin your summit hike from the highest base camp around midnight so that you summit at sunrise. The air is a little easier to breathe at night so there is a method to the madness. Anyway, it can actually get below zero, so I snagged some electrics. I was by far the most comfortable hiker at 4:00am when it got really cold. I had them on high and ran through the battery in four hours, but the warmth stuck around for another few hours which was perfect because then the sun started coming up by then.
